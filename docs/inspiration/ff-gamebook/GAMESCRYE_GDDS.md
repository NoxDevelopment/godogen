## JOB 3 — Best inspiration picks for narrative / RPG / branching-adventure design

From the gamescrye set, the strongest references for ff-gamebook's lane:

1. **Planescape: Torment** (#14, Black Isle CRPG doc) — the gold standard for prose-dense,
   choice-driven RPG writing where dialogue and consequence outweigh combat; closest in spirit
   to authoring branching gamebook passages.
2. **Grim Fandango** (#41, Tim Schafer) — its item/action dependency web is exactly the
   "puzzle graph / gating" model a branching adventure needs to stay solvable-but-not-obvious.
3. **Diablo — pitch** (#4, Brevik) — a tight, fundable ARPG pitch stating core loop and scope
   as convictions; the template for pitching a stat-driven adventure before it's built.
4. **Fallout 2: Brotherhood of Steel** (#36) — SPECIAL-style stat-gated choices and branching
   outcomes; a direct analog to SKILL/STAMINA/LUCK checks driving gamebook branches.
5. **Metal Gear Solid 2 — Grand Game Plan** (#13, Kojima) — a masterclass in stating theme and
   narrative intent up front so every system serves the story; the "vision-first" discipline our
   GDD should emulate.
